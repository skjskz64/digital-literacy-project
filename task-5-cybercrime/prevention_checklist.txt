STAY SAFE ONLINE
PREVENTION CHECKLIST 

(FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS IN INDIA)

- Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone, even if they
  claim to be from a bank or payment app.

- Remember: To receive money, you do NOT need to enter 
  your UPI PIN. Entering a PIN always means money is being 
  sent.

- Verify unknown links and QR codes before scanning or 
  clicking. Avoid links from strangers or suspicious messages.

- Do not accept random “collect requests” on UPI apps  
  unless you are sure about the sender.

- Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
  (2FA) for all apps, especially banking and email.

- Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions, as it may not  
  be secure.

- Keep your apps updated to the latest version to stay
  protected from security vulnerabilities.

- Be cautious on social media - do not share personal details
  like phone number, address, or bank information publicly.

- Install apps only from official stores (Google Play Store/App 
  Store) to avoid malware or fake apps.

- Regularly check your bank statements and transaction 
   history to detect any unauthorized activity  early.

REPORTING CYBERCRIME IN INDIA

National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in?
Helpline Number: 1930

If you face any fraud, report it immediately to increase the chances of recovering your money.
